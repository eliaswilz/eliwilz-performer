Cardinal Bar isn't just where I perform every Thursday — it's where I learned what it means to read a room. Performing between jazz sets teaches you timing in a way that's impossible to replicate. The audience is there for music, so you have to earn their attention in a completely different way.

The Ivory Room feels like someone's living room, if someone's living room happened to have a stage and a full bar. It's where comics go to try material that might be too weird or personal for bigger rooms. The intimacy forces you to be more conversational, more honest.

Comedy Club on State is the closest thing Madison has to a traditional comedy club, and that's exactly what the scene needs. The lighting, the brick walls, the two-drink minimum — it all creates expectations that push you to deliver tighter, more professional sets.

High Noon Saloon books comedy occasionally, and when they do, it's always an event. The room is huge compared to our usual spots, which means you have to project energy differently. It's good practice for anyone thinking about taking their act on the road.

Each venue teaches you something different about your craft, and Madison's lucky to have this variety in such a small scene.
