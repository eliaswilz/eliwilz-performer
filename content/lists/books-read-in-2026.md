### Game of Thrones
I tried to read this book back in college when I was actually watching the series Game of Thrones. Talk to any fan of the show and they'll let you know how much better the early seasons are because they're backed by the writing of George RR Martin, who hadn't finished the book series by the time the TV show ended. I agree, and unfortunately back then, I agreed too much. I found the book hard to get through because it so closely followed the plot of the TV show, I was bored by the fact that I'd seen this before. It does however, make for a great review 10 years later. I haven't watched an episode of Game of Thrones since the finale, so I enjoyed revisiting the world of Westeros. I wasn't so initmately familar with the plot that it was enjoyable to swoop back in, but I obviously remember the big beats of the show, and found myself dreading the end as we got closer and closer to death. I will probably pick up book 2 at some point, I've heard they slowly diverge more and more from the TV show, so it will be fun to read.

### No Country for Old Men
My favorite movie for a long time, I'd bought the book years ago and never actually picked it up. Written by Cormac McCarthy it's set near the Texas-Mexico border and one of those where the setting is enjoyable and immerses you in the world in which it takes place. The style in which it was written, with the monologues from the sherriff setting up these broad themes really makes him feel like the main character of the book in a way the movie I think deliberately misleads you on by centering Llewellyn. It's one of those movies I've watched 100 times and find something different to latch on to, the book has been the same.
